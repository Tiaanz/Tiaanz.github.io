Foundations Reflection

1. What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

I have identified my strengths and weaknesses. I believe that I could do anything I want with my perseverance and problem-solving skills. Also, it is so relieving to know the power of Neuroplasticity and growth mindset. I am not a smart person, but I can still achieve my goal as a developer with effort and practice. Last but not the least, the core learning introduced me mindfulness, which can be very helpful to overcome obstacles and frustrations.

2. What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Values are a person’s belief in what is right and what is wrong. They guide us to do things right and keep us on the right track of learning.
Empathy helps us to understand others and foster collaboration within a team, which is important for group learning and future employment.
Self-awareness makes us know our strengths and limitations and make the most use of our strengths to achieve the goal.

3. What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

I think it was neuroplasticity and growth mindset, as there has always been a stereotype that only clever people can code. These concepts really motivated me and gave me confidence in myself.

4. What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

The most challenging part was to explore myself to find out my strengths and limitations, my values and my identity. To be honest, I have not thought much about it before, but it was good that I had the chance to think about it and had a deeper understanding of myself.

5. Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

To make us amazing human beings, hahaha. As we graduate and step into the workplace, we are not just being there to do some certain tasks, but also we are bringing ourselves to the team. What we are and what we do can influence as well as contribute to the team's success.

2. In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

I am impressed with the power of growth mindset. Most of us are not born with high IQ or are extremely intelligent, however, it does not stop us from achieving success and happiness if we believe that our abilities can be developed through effort.

3. How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?

Whenever I encounter difficulties and want to run away, I would tell myself that it is ok to get struggled, just take your time and keep trying and you will get it sorted.

4. Link to a resource that you found particularly useful or engaging.

Stanford University’s Carol Dweck on the Growth Mindset and Education
